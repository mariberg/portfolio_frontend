Tilawa — Quran Study Companion

Type
Hackathon
Role
Solo — Full Stack
Bedrock Nova Lite Lambda DynamoDB CloudFormation Flutter AI Personalisation OAuth2 PKCE
The problem

Muslims reciting the Quran often have limited context about what they're about to read — especially those still developing their Arabic. Generic apps provide translations but nothing personalised to the reader's proficiency level or reading history.

The approach

Tilawa generates a personalised AI briefing before each recitation session — a contextual summary and vocabulary flashcards filtered to words the user hasn't encountered before, based on their Arabic level and DynamoDB reading history.

Architecture CloudFormation
Key decisions
  • Cross-account STS for Bedrock — Lambda assumes a role in a separate AWS account. Enterprise-grade security, AI costs isolated and auditable.
  • Two-pass keyword filtering — vocabulary filtered by Arabic level first, then against user history. Genuinely novel flashcards each session.
  • Single-table DynamoDB — user sessions, history, and vocab colocated with PK/SK patterns. Requires discipline upfront, scales cleanly.
  • Property-based testing both sides — fast-check (Node.js) and glados (Flutter). Uncommon in hackathon projects.
